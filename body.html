Indigenous Roots & Early Contact

For millennia, Indigenous peoples have lived on this land. The 1600s saw the arrival of European explorers and the fur trade, leading to the establishment of New France and British colonies.

Confederation (1867)

On July 1, 1867, the British North America Act joined Ontario, Quebec, New Brunswick, and Nova Scotia into the Dominion of Canada.

A Growing Nation

From the World Wars to the Charter of Rights and Freedoms in 1982, Canada has evolved into a diverse, multicultural parliamentary democracy.
